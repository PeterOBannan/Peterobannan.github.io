The Advanced Recovery Coalition (ARC)

Neutralizing Hazards. Securing Resources. Transforming Infrastructure.

About ARC

The Advanced Recovery Coalition (ARC) is a regional 501(c)(3) non-profit initiative dedicated to solving two of the most critical infrastructure challenges of the 21st century: the environmental threat of hazardous municipal waste leachates and the fragile domestic supply chain for critical, rare-earth, and defense-grade metals.

By integrating cutting-edge thermal extraction technologies into existing municipal Waste-to-Energy (WTE) infrastructure, ARC aims to convert high-liability hazardous ash into an inert, safe resource while harvesting the high-value metals locked within.

The SpARC Project: Spokane Pilot

Project Sp-ARC (Spokane Advanced Recovery) is our flagship pilot focused on igniting more use of waste-to-energy facilities. These facilities offer the power needed to run the processing—providing the most clean energy possible, re-cycling metal, and upcycling final waste into sequestered concrete.

The Science: Advanced Thermal Remediation

ARC’s operational model relies on the localized deployment of Flash Joule Heating (FJH) paired with Electrothermal Chlorination (ETC).

  • Biological Destruction: Material reaches 3,000°C in milliseconds, instantly severing carbon-carbon bonds of organic toxins (dioxins, furans, and PFAS).
  • Chemical Extraction: High-value metals bind with chlorine to form metal chlorides, which "flash" out as gas to be harvested, leaving behind inert ash for structural concrete.

Strategic Roadmap

Phase I: Coalition Assembly (Current) - Finalizing 501(c)(3) and academic partnerships.

Phase II: Ash Characterization - Mapping critical metal PPM via ICP-MS with university partners.

Phase III: 1-TPD Pilot Deployment - Deploying a modular 1-Ton-Per-Day FJH reactor adjacent to WTE infrastructure.

Phase IV: Scaling - Creating a scalable blueprint for municipalities nationwide.
