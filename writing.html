Vision & Core Philosophies

True leadership requires a clear, articulated stance on complex issues. Below are my operational philosophies and visions for the key areas of infrastructure, education, institutional reform, and family that drive my work.

Education & School Systems

My educational approach centers on fostering an inclusive, dynamic learning environment that encourages curiosity and scientific literacy[cite: 157]. I believe in active, mastery-based learning that utilizes hands-on experiments, real-world problem-solving, and interdisciplinary approaches to bridge complex concepts[cite: 158].

Beyond the classroom, I am highly committed to equity and affordability in higher education[cite: 159]. By aggressively advocating for open-access resources and AI-enhanced tools, we can minimize financial barriers and costs, specifically easing the pressure faced by first-generation learners[cite: 159].

Eastern State Hospital (ESH) & Institutional Accountability

Large bureaucracies often default to protecting themselves rather than their personnel or constituents. At facilities like ESH, this manifests as a toxic culture where staff routinely hide behind job titles to avoid liability[cite: 102, 103]. To dismantle these failing structures, a traumatic critical intervention is required to re-instill personhood and accountability into the system[cite: 217].

My vision for institutional reform relies on radical audits, digital transparency, and physical accountability—such as mandatory visible names to completely strip the anonymity of job titles[cite: 219, 220, 221]. Only after rigid rules and absolute liability are established can a healthy, individualized organizational culture be reintroduced[cite: 222].

Advanced Infrastructure & Joule Heating

My ultimate aspiration is to act as a bridge connecting vast, underutilized macro-level resources with the desperate, granular needs of local communities[cite: 37, 187]. Projects like the integration of Flash Joule Heating for waste-to-energy municipal systems represent the peak of this philosophy.

By applying rigorous cost-benefit analysis and cognitive pattern recognition, we can identify and solve systemic failures before they manifest as environmental or financial crises[cite: 37, 185]. Just as physical insulation protects a home, implementing cutting-edge remediation technologies allows us to systemically "insulate" and protect our civic infrastructure from breaking down[cite: 175].

Family & Origins

I was born the sixth of nine children in the poorest areas of Spokane, an environment that forced me to intuitively grasp complex systems simply by observing the continuous variations within my own highly populated household[cite: 4, 6, 23]. Today, my wife Tawnee and I are raising a blended household of four children[cite: 9].

The rhythms of family, faith, and small-town life in Medical Lake completely anchor me[cite: 561, 562, 563]. Navigating high-conflict personal challenges to protect my children has heavily reinforced my resilience and my absolute commitment to radical authenticity[cite: 184, 186, 204]. Ultimately, my drive to reform institutions and innovate infrastructure is to ensure that the systems my children inherit are transparent, accountable, and functional.
