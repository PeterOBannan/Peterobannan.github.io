Origins & Family Foundation

I am a Spokane native, born on January 5, 1990, as the sixth of nine children in the North Central area. Growing up in a large household, I developed an intuitive grasp of human variance and systems logic simply by observing the diverse trajectories of my siblings. This city shaped my values, and I have dedicated my career to giving back to the community that built me.

Today, my compass and greatest motivation is my family. I am happily remarried, living in Medical Lake, WA, with my brilliant wife, Tawnee. Together, we lead a blended household of four incredible children (ages 11, 10, 10, and 8). Every strategic project I take on—whether it's environmental recovery or institutional reform—is ultimately about building a safer, more sustainable future for them.

A Teacher at Heart

My career didn't begin in boardrooms or industrial pilot plants; it started in the classroom. For over six years, I served as a STEM educator, teaching Chemistry and Robotics. It was there that I learned the foundational skills of leadership: how to listen, how to break down highly complex problems into digestible concepts, and how to empower others to succeed.

Though my title has shifted into macro-level institutional leadership, my identity has not. I am still a teacher at heart. The empathy, patience, and strategic planning I honed as an educator are the exact tools I use today to build coalitions between academics, municipal leaders, and industry vendors.

The "External RAM" Methodology

In my operational life, I operate with Multimodal Aphantasia, which limits internal visual synthesis but maximizes high-speed external processing. To leverage this, I utilize a specific "External Dialogue" workflow: raw data is processed through AI synthesis and externalized logic models.

This bypasses internal working memory limitations, functioning almost like "External RAM." It allows me to rapidly synthesize complex legal, chemical, and operational data into actionable, transparent strategies, ensuring that ambitious projects like ARC move efficiently from concept to execution.
