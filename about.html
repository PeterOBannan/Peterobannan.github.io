Origins & The Family Compass

Born in 1990 as the sixth of nine children, I grew up in a large household in a lower-income area of Spokane. That environment taught me the value of community, resilience, and looking out for one another. This city shaped my foundational values, and my ultimate professional aspiration is to act as a bridge, connecting underutilized institutional resources with the granular needs of our local communities.

Today, my absolute compass is my family. I live in Medical Lake, WA, with my wife, Tawnee, and our blended household of four incredible children. Our oldest, an 11-year-old son entering middle school, shares my intellect and deep care for people; he is a believer, an encourager, and a kid with a true bias for action. Our two 10-year-old daughters have been "thick as thieves" since the day they met, growing into passionate, strong, and driven young women. Rounding out the crew is our youngest—a brilliant and beautiful 8-year-old daughter who is always observing and learning from her older siblings.

Every complex project I undertake—from environmental recovery to institutional reform—is driven by a personal motivation to show them that structural community problems can be solved.

The Heart of an Educator

My career didn't begin in boardrooms or industrial pilot plants; it is firmly rooted in the classroom. Armed with a B.A. in Chemistry, I served as a STEM educator across Eastern Washington for over six years, teaching chemistry, biology, mathematics, and robotics.

During my time in the school system, my priority was always balancing high academic rigor with deep empathy for diverse learners, advocating relentlessly for vulnerable students. Though my title has shifted into macro-level leadership, my identity has not. I am still a teacher at heart. The empathy, patience, and ability to break down highly complex problems into digestible concepts are the exact tools I use today to build coalitions between academics, municipal leaders, and industry vendors.

Strategic Leadership & Advocacy

My transition from the classroom to institutional leadership was a natural evolution of my desire to serve. I realized that to truly protect vulnerable populations, we must repair the broken bureaucracies that oversee them. This drive led me to earn my M.S. in Management and Leadership, and I am currently completing my Ph.D. in Strategic Leadership.

Today, my professional objective is to operate as a people-first, growth-minded leader. Whether I am utilizing Lean management principles to refine workplace cultures or leading the Advanced Recovery Coalition to solve municipal waste crises, my methodology remains the same: prioritize objective truth, dismantle toxic systems, and ensure that every organizational action serves the community.
