Books

The purpose of this section is simple: to share a few books that have stood out in my own thinking. It’s a slim slice of many titles I enjoy, especially on audiobook. I take book suggestions seriously—if something has been meaningful to you, I’d love to hear about it. Please send recommendations via the contact page.

(At some point I may add a separate list of books I’ve paused or not yet finished— but for now, this page focuses on selections I’ve found interesting and meaningful.)

A small, meaningful shelf

  • The Holy Bible — canonical texts collected over centuries
  • Les Misérables — Victor Hugo
  • How to Win Friends and Influence People — Dale Carnegie
  • Think and Grow Rich — Napoleon Hill
  • The Mysterious Stranger — Mark Twain
  • The 21 Irrefutable Laws of Leadership — John C. Maxwell
  • The Kite Runner — Khaled Hosseini

Want to swap notes or suggest a title? Send me a message.

A question for you

Think of all the characters you’ve encountered in any media—books, TV, film, games. Which character do you most identify as being the opposite of yourself, and why?

If you’d like to share your answer, drop it in a note on the contact page. I’m always curious how stories shape us.
